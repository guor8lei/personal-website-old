About Me

Hi, I'm Raymond! I am a sophomore at the University of California, Berkeley majoring in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS).


Ever since high school, I've been fascinated by computer science and its far-reaching impacts. Ever since, I have been constantly exploring its many facets, from software development, to information technology, to research. In particular, I'm also interested in applying potential of computer science to biology and genetics.


I'm proficient in Python and Java, being a Oracle Certified Associate, Java SE 8 Programmer. I'm also familiar with shell scripting as well as web development frameworks such as React and Django.


I have had previous work experience at multiple companies and organizations, including Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory (Network Engineer Intern), Berkeley CodeBase (Software Developer), National Institutes of Health (Bioinformatics Research Intern), BidBox, LLC (Mobile App Developer), and the University of Maryland (Computational Geography Research Intern).


Through these experiences, I have written a published published research paper, was awarded as a Regeneron Science Talent Search (STS) semifinalist, and coded an Android app.


Outside of academics, I love watching movies and playing ice hockey. When I have spare time, I write in a small blog, where I post my thoughts on movies.
